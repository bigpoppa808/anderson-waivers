Elasticity Problem

A theater faces demand $Q_d = 500 - 10P$. Current price is $30.

  • Calculate elasticity at $P = 30$
  • Should they raise or lower price to increase revenue?

Solution:

At $P = 30$: $Q = 500 - 10(30) = 200$

$$E_d = \frac{dQ}{dP} \times \frac{P}{Q} = -10 \times \frac{30}{200} = -1.5$$

Since $|E_d| = 1.5 > 1$, demand is elastic.

Decision: Lower price to increase revenue (elastic demand means $\downarrow P \rightarrow \uparrow TR$)

Monopoly Pricing

Monopolist faces: $P = 100 - Q$ with $MC = 20$

Find profit-maximizing $P$ and $Q$.

Solution:

Total Revenue: $TR = P \times Q = (100 - Q)Q = 100Q - Q^2$

$$MR = \frac{dTR}{dQ} = 100 - 2Q$$

Set $MR = MC$:

$$100 - 2Q = 20 \Rightarrow Q^* = 40$$

Price: $P^* = 100 - 40 = 60$

Profit: $\pi = (P - MC) \times Q = (60 - 20) \times 40 = 1600$

Cournot Competition

Two firms face $P = 100 - Q$ where $Q = q_1 + q_2$, with $MC = 10$

Solution:

Using Cournot formula for symmetric firms:

$$q_i = \frac{a - c}{3b} = \frac{100 - 10}{3 \times 1} = 30$$

Total output: $Q = 60$

Price: $P = 100 - 60 = 40$

Profit per firm: $\pi_i = (40 - 10) \times 30 = 900$

Tax Incidence

Demand: $Q_d = 100 - 2P$

Supply: $Q_s = -20 + 3P$

Tax: $t = 4$ per unit on sellers

Solution:

Without tax: Set $Q_d = Q_s$

$$100 - 2P = -20 + 3P \Rightarrow P^* = 24, Q^* = 52$$

With tax: Supply shifts to $Q_s = -20 + 3(P - 4) = -32 + 3P$

$$100 - 2P = -32 + 3P \Rightarrow P_c = 26.4$$

Quantity: $Q = 47.2$

Producer receives: $P_s = 26.4 - 4 = 22.4$

Burden: Consumers pay $+2.4$, Producers bear $-1.6$

Game Theory: Nash Equilibrium

Prisoner's Dilemma Payoff Matrix:

Cooperate Defect
Cooperate (3, 3) (0, 5)
Defect (5, 0) (1, 1)

Solution:

Player 1's best responses:

  • If Player 2 cooperates: $5 > 3$ → Defect
  • If Player 2 defects: $1 > 0$ → Defect

By symmetry, Player 2 also chooses Defect.

Nash Equilibrium: (Defect, Defect) with payoffs (1, 1)

Note: This is inefficient since (3, 3) Pareto dominates (1, 1)

Multiplier Effect

If $MPC = 0.8$ and government spending increases by $\$100$ billion:

Solution:

$$\text{Multiplier} = \frac{1}{1 - MPC} = \frac{1}{1 - 0.8} = 5$$

Total GDP increase: $\$100B \times 5 = \$500$ billion
